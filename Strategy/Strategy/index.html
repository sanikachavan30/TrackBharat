Tracking India's Progress Towards the UN 2030 Goals

1. What are SDGs?

Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) are 17 global goals adopted by all UN member states in 2015 to address critical issues such as poverty, inequality, health, education, climate change, and sustainability. They form part of the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development, aiming to promote peace and prosperity for all.

2. Why is tracking them important?

Tracking SDGs is essential for measuring progress, identifying gaps, and ensuring accountability. It helps governments and policymakers make data-driven decisions, allocate resources efficiently, and ensure that no one is left behind.

3. What is India doing?

India is actively aligning its national agenda with the SDGs. The NITI Aayog, in partnership with ministries and states, has launched the SDG India Index to track progress across all 17 goals. This encourages competitive and cooperative federalism and supports localized action planning.

4. Source: NITI Aayog, SDG India Index, UNDP

Data is primarily sourced from the NITI Aayog’s SDG India Index, created with support from the United Nations Development Programme (UNDP). The index uses a wide set of national indicators to monitor development, guide interventions, and highlight challenges.

Department of Economic and Social Affairs
Sustainable Development

The 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development, adopted by all United Nations Member States in 2015, provides a shared blueprint for peace and prosperity for people and the planet, now and into the future. At its heart are the 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), which are an urgent call for action by all countries - developed and developing - in a global partnership.

THE 17 GOALS

NO POVERTY

End poverty in all its forms everywhere. Eradicate extreme poverty and reduce the proportion of people living below national poverty lines.

ZERO HUNGER

End hunger, achieve food security, improve nutrition, and promote sustainable agriculture. Ensure all people, especially children, have access to sufficient and nutritious food all year round.

GOOD HEALTH AND WELL-BEING

Ensure healthy lives and promote well-being for all at all ages. Reduce maternal and child mortality, combat diseases, ensure universal health coverage.

QUALITY EDUCATION

Ensure inclusive and equitable quality education and promote lifelong learning opportunities for all. Provide free primary and secondary education, eliminate gender disparities, and improve adult literacy.

GENDER EQUALITY

Achieve gender equality and empower all women and girls. End discrimination, violence, and harmful practices; ensure equal leadership and participation.

CLEAN WATER AND SANITATION

Ensure availability and sustainable management of water and sanitation for all. Achieve universal access to safe and affordable drinking water and adequate sanitation.

Strategic Initiatives for a Sustainable India
Initiative 01

Skill India Mission

A national initiative to equip over 40 crore people with market-relevant skills. It promotes employment, entrepreneurship, and upskilling, especially among youth and women, thus supporting inclusive economic growth.

Initiative 02

National Action Plan on Climate Change (NAPCC)

A multi-pronged initiative to address climate mitigation and adaptation through missions focused on solar energy, energy efficiency, water, and green India. It supports India's transition to a low-carbon economy.

Initiative 03

Ayushman Bharat – Pradhan Mantri Jan Arogya Yojana (PM-JAY)

World’s largest health insurance scheme offering ₹5 lakh per family per year for secondary and tertiary care. It ensures universal health coverage and reduces the financial burden of healthcare on poor families.

Initiative 04

Digital India Mission

Launched to transform India into a digitally empowered society and knowledge economy, this initiative promotes digital infrastructure, online services, and digital literacy. It supports inclusive development by bridging the urban-rural digital divide and enhancing e-governance.

Do you know all 17 SDGs?

The 17 Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), adopted by all United Nations member states in 2015, aim to create a better and more sustainable future for all by 2030. These goals address global challenges such as poverty, hunger, inequality, climate change, clean water, education, and health. They promote peace, justice, economic growth, innovation, and responsible consumption. Each goal is interconnected, requiring global partnerships and local action. India has embraced the SDGs through national policies and initiatives that align development with sustainability. By empowering communities, encouraging innovation, and ensuring inclusive growth, India contributes actively to achieving these global goals.

From Policy to Impact: India’s Contribution to Global SDGs Policy

NITI Aayog (National Institution for Transforming India)

NITI Aayog is the nodal agency for coordinating India’s SDG implementation. It prepares the SDG India Index, which tracks the progress of all states and Union Territories across 17 goals. The agency works on data-driven policymaking, encouraging innovation, sustainable economic growth, and partnerships. It helps integrate the SDGs into national strategies and supports state-level SDG localization. NITI Aayog bridges global commitments with India’s domestic development priorities.

Ministry of Education

The Ministry of Education plays a critical role in achieving Goal 4: Quality Education. It drives key initiatives such as the National Education Policy (NEP) 2020, Samagra Shiksha, and Digital India e-learning platforms. The ministry works to ensure inclusive and equitable education for all by improving school infrastructure, reducing dropout rates, and promoting digital literacy. Vocational training and skill development programs also align with SDG 8 (Decent Work and Economic Growth).

Ministry of Health and Family Welfare

The Ministry of Health and Family Welfare leads efforts toward Goal 3: Good Health and Well-being. Through initiatives like Ayushman Bharat (PM-JAY), National Health Mission, and Universal Immunization Programme, the ministry aims to provide affordable and accessible healthcare for all. It also promotes maternal and child health, disease control, mental health awareness, and health infrastructure strengthening, especially in rural and underserved areas.

Government of Gujarat

The Government of Gujarat has taken a proactive role in localizing the SDGs by integrating them into the state’s development agenda. It focuses on clean energy, women's empowerment, healthcare access, and rural upliftment. Gujarat has launched state-specific initiatives under renewable energy (Goal 7), industrial innovation (Goal 9), and clean water and sanitation (Goal 6). Its performance in the SDG India Index has often ranked it among the leading states.
